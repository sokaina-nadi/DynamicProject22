MOORE & HODGSON

In1968, J. M. Moore [5] proposed an O (n log n) algorithm for minimizing the number of late jobs on a single machine. Moore stated “The algorithm developed in this paper, however, consists of only two sorting operations performed on the total set of jobs, . . . Consequently, this method will be computationally feasible for very large problems and can be performed manually on many smaller problems.” Step1: Sequence by earliest due date EDD d[1] < d[2] < d[3] <…< d[n] Step2: Find the 1st tardy job in the current sequence, say job i. IF None exists go to Step 4 Step3: Consider jobs [1]. [2],... [i] Reject the job with largest ti and Return to Step2. Step4: Current sequence + rejected job (In any order.)


The Moore-Hodgson Algorithm perform well for small and large data sizes!
